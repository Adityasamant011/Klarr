Shopify Profit Margin Calculator: How to Find Your Real Margin

June 13, 2026 · 8 min read · Finance

Most Shopify stores think they know their profit margin. They don't.

Revenue looks great on the dashboard. But after transaction fees, refunds, app costs, and ad spend... the real margin is usually 15-20 points lower than founders think.

We built Klarr to solve this. But even if you're not ready for bookkeeping yet, you can calculate your real margin today.

The Problem: Your Dashboard Lies

Shopify shows you revenue. Not profit.

It doesn't show you:

  • Payment processing fees (2.9% + $0.30 per transaction)
  • Refunds (3-5% of revenue for most stores)
  • App subscriptions ($200-500/month for the average store)
  • Shipping label costs
  • Chargebacks
  • Ad spend

When you add all these up, a store doing $10K/mo with a "40% margin" is often actually at 18-25%.

How to Calculate Your Real Profit Margin

Here's the formula:

Real Profit Margin = (Revenue - ALL Costs) / Revenue × 100

Not just COGS. ALL costs.

Step 1: Get Your Revenue Numbers

From Shopify Admin → Analytics → Overview:

  • Gross sales
  • Returns/refunds
  • Net sales = Gross sales - Returns

Step 2: Add Up ALL Your Costs

Step 3: Do the Math

Here's a real example from a store we analyzed:

Revenue:           $50,000
COGS:             -$20,000
Transaction fees:  -$1,450
Refunds:           -$2,500
App costs:         -$3,600
Ad spend:         -$15,000
Shipping:          -$4,000
─────────────────────────
Real profit:        $3,450
Real margin:        6.9%

They thought they were making 40%. The real number was 6.9%.

The Hidden Costs Most Stores Miss

1. Transaction Fees

On a $100K/yr store, that's $2,900+ in fees alone. Most founders don't track this as a separate line item.

2. Refund Costs

It's not just the refund amount. You also lose the transaction fee on the original sale, plus the cost of the product (if it can't be resold).

3. App Subscription Creep

The average Shopify store uses 10-15 apps. At $20-50 each, that's $200-750/month in app costs alone.

4. Ad Spend

This is the big one. Most stores spend 15-30% of revenue on ads. If you're not including this in your margin calculation, you're not calculating margin.
