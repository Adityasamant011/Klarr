The $50K Problem

Let me tell you about a conversation I keep having with Shopify founders.

They tell me: "We did $50,000 in revenue last month. Our margins are great — around 40%."

I ask: "What's in that 40%?"

They say: "Revenue minus product cost. We buy the product for $12, sell for $45. That's 73% gross margin. After ad spend, we're at 40% net."

I say: "What about Shopify fees? Payment processing? Packaging? Fulfillment? Returns? Chargebacks? App fees? Currency conversion?"

Silence.

This is the most common financial mistake in DTC. Founders calculate their margin using 2-3 line items and ignore the other 10-15 costs that eat into profit. The result? They think they're profitable when they're actually breaking even — or losing money.

What Shopify Shows You (And Why It's Misleading)

Shopify's dashboard is designed to make you feel good. It shows you:

  • Gross sales: Total customer payments before any deductions
  • Net sales: Gross sales minus refunds and discounts
  • Average order value: Net sales divided by order count

What Shopify doesn't show you:

  • Payment processing fees (2.9% + $0.30 per transaction)
  • App transaction fees (Recharge, Afterpay, etc.)
  • COGS (product cost, packaging, shipping)
  • Fulfillment fees (3PL costs)
  • Ad spend (Meta, Google, TikTok)
  • Return costs (return shipping, restocking)
  • Chargeback fees ($15-25 per dispute)
  • Sales tax remittance costs
  • Currency conversion spreads

Shopify shows you revenue. It doesn't show you profit. And for a DTC brand, the gap between revenue and profit is massive.

This is why accurate bookkeeping isn't just an accounting exercise. It's the difference between thinking your business is healthy and actually knowing.

7 Hidden Costs That Destroy DTC Margins

1. Payment processing fees. 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction sounds small. On $50K in sales, that's $1,450-1,750/month. If you also accept PayPal (2.99% + $0.49), it's even more.

2. App transaction fees. Recharge charges $0.19 per subscription renewal. Afterpay charges a percentage. If you have 5 apps with per-transaction fees, you could be paying $500-800/month in app fees alone.

3. Return processing costs. A returned product isn't just lost revenue. You pay return shipping ($5-8), restocking labor ($2-3), and the product may not be resellable (damaged packaging). Average cost per return: $10-15. At a 5% return rate on 1,000 orders, that's $500-750/month.

4. Chargebacks. Each chargeback costs $15-25 in fees, plus the lost revenue. If you have 10 chargebacks/month (typical for a $50K/mo store), that's $150-250 in fees plus $500-1,000 in lost revenue.

5. Currency conversion spreads. If 20% of your sales are international, Shopify's currency conversion fee (1-2%) costs you $200-400/month. Using a multi-currency account like Wise can cut this in half.

6. Fulfillment creep. Your 3PL charges $3.50 per pick/pack. But they also charge for storage ($15-25/pallet/month), receiving ($25-40/pallet), and special services (poly bagging, inserts, kitting). These "extras" add $300-600/month.

7. Ad platform fees. Meta and Google don't charge fees on ad spend, but if you use a management tool (Triple Whale, Northbeam), that's $100-300/month. Plus, your actual ROAS might be lower than reported due to attribution gaps.

If your margins are below these ranges, you're either underpricing, overspending on ads, or not tracking all your costs. If you're above them, congratulations — but make sure you're actually counting everything.

How to Fix Your Margin Calculation

Here's a 4-step process to get your real margin number:

Step 1: Export everything. Export your Shopify payouts, bank statements, ad platform reports, and 3PL invoices for the last 3 months.

Step 2: List every cost. Go through every expense — not just the obvious ones. Include app fees, chargebacks, return shipping, fulfillment extras, currency conversion, everything.

Step 3: Calculate monthly. Don't look at annual numbers. Monthly fluctuations hide problems. A bad month might be an outlier — or it might be the new normal.

Step 4: Compare to benchmarks. Use the ranges above. If you're below benchmark, find the gap. It's usually one of the 7 hidden costs listed above.
