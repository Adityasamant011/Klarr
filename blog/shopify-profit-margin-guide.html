They look at their Shopify dashboard, see $50,000 in revenue, subtract COGS, and think they have a 60% margin. They're wrong. By a lot. ## The Problem: Shopify Shows You the Wrong Numbers Your Shopify dashboard shows net payouts, not revenue. The gap between these two numbers is where most founders lose track of their real profitability. Here's what a typical month looks like: | Metric | Amount | |--------|--------| | Gross Sales | $50,000 | | Shopify Transaction Fees | -$1,450 | | Payment Processing | -$1,200 | | Refunds | -$800 | | Chargebacks | -$350 | | App Fees | -$200 | | **Net Payout** | **$46,000** | | | | | COGS | -$20,000 | | Ad Spend | -$10,000 | | **Real Profit** | **$15,400** | | **Real Margin** | **30.8%** | Most founders would calculate their margin as: ``` ($50,000 - $20,000 COGS) / $50,000 = 60% margin ``` But the REAL margin is: ``` ($50,000 - $20,000 COGS - $3,800 Fees - $800 Refunds - $10,000 Ads) / $50,000 = 30.8% margin ``` **That's half of what they think they're making.** ## How to Calculate Your Real Profit Margin ### The Formula ``` Real Margin = (Gross Sales - COGS - All Fees - Refunds - Ad Spend) / Gross Sales ``` ### What to Include **Gross Sales**: Total sales before any fees or refunds **COGS**: Cost of goods sold (product cost + shipping) **All Fees**: - Shopify transaction fees (2.9% + 30¢ per transaction) - Payment processing fees (2-3%) - App fees (Klaviyo, Judge.me, etc.) - Any other platform fees **Refunds**: Returned orders **Ad Spend**: Meta, Google, TikTok, influencer costs ### Common Mistakes 1. **Using net payouts instead of gross sales** — inflates your margin 2. **Forgetting app fees** — can add up to $200-500/month 3. **Not accounting for refunds** — especially during holiday season 4. **Ignoring ad spend** — this is often the biggest expense after COGS ## Free Profit Margin Calculator We built a free tool that calculates your real profit margin in 2 minutes.
