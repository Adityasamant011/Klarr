The answer isn't always straightforward. It depends on your revenue, complexity, time, and confidence in your numbers. In this guide, we'll help you figure out when it's time to get help. ## The DIY Stage Most Shopify founders start with DIY bookkeeping. And that's fine — if you're doing it right. ### DIY Works When: - You're doing <$50K/month in sales - You have a simple product line - You sell primarily on Shopify - You're comfortable with spreadsheets - You have time to do it properly (5+ hours/month) - You understand basic accounting principles ### DIY Tools You Need: - QuickBooks or Xero - A2X (Shopify to QuickBooks integration) - A spreadsheet for tracking COGS - A system for reconciling payouts ## The Warning Signs You need help when: ### 1. You Spend More Than 5 Hours/Month on Bookkeeping If you're spending a full day or more on bookkeeping, it's time to outsource. Your time is better spent on growth. ### 2. You're Not Confident in Your Numbers If you can't answer "Am I actually profitable?" with confidence, you need help. ### 3. You're Behind on Reconciliation If you haven't reconciled your Shopify payouts in 2+ months, you're flying blind. ### 4. Tax Season Is Stressful If you dread tax season because your books are a mess, it's time to get help. ### 5. You're Scaling Fast If you're growing >20% month-over-month, your bookkeeping complexity grows even faster. ## The Cost of DIY vs Hiring ### DIY Costs: - QuickBooks: $30/month - A2X: $20/month - Your time: 5-10 hours/month - **Total: $50-100/month + your time** ### Hiring a Bookkeeper: - Klarr: $149/month - Your time: 0 hours - **Total: $149/month** ### The Math: If you value your time at $100/hour and you spend 5 hours/month on bookkeeping, that's $500/month in opportunity cost.
